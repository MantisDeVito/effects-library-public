Transitions

Cuts that do a job. Every transition loops A → B → A so you see the effect land and unwind. Whip pans for energy, luma wipes for editorial polish, iris for drama, dip-to-white for time jumps. Avoid stock liquid transitions un-retimed and un-recoloured — they're the #1 AI slop signal. Retime, recolour, or build your own.

Hit transitions

Hit punch
Hard scale punch with frame-shake decay. Heavier than zoom-punch — the shake holds for 3 frames after impact.
Hit shake — vertical
Pure Y-axis earthquake. Cut hides inside the worst frame.
Hit shake — horizontal
Pure X-axis ratchet. Reads sharper than vertical because eyes track left-right motion as faster.
Hit shake — roll
Wobble. Translate plus rotation oscillation. Reads like a handheld camera taking the punch.
Hit RGB kick
Pure chromatic channel split. Red shifts right, blue shifts left, green holds. Cut hides inside the split chaos.
Hit spin
60° rotation kick at the cut, motion blur masks the swap. Frame shake decays out.
Hit spin + RGB
90° rotation combined with heavy chromatic split. The most aggressive of the rotation hits.
Hit glitch
Chromatic split + scanlines + frame shake. For moments where the cut should feel like the edit broke the signal.
Hit stretch
Horizontal squeeze. Frame compresses to a thin slice and pops back. Hard sideways punch.
Hit squash
Vertical squash. Frame collapses to a thin band. Pairs with a sub-bass hit.
Hit bounce
Anticipation drop. Frame lifts up, slams down, bounce-and-settle. Vertical drop hit.
Hit jelly
Jelly-roll wobble. Scale oscillation overshoots, undershoots, settles. Playful, bouncy beat.
Hit flash impact
Heavier than the standard flash. Radial bloom holds for 4 frames, decays through frame shake. Real impact, not a strobe.
Hit anamorphic
Horizontal lens-flare streak rips across the frame at the cut. Cinematic action-trailer feel.
Hit invert
Single-frame colour invert at impact. Held shake. Subtle but reads as a hard cut, especially with sub-bass.
Hit distortion zoom
Heavy radial blur smear over a scale punch. Distortion masks the cut. Sells "we just punched through this scene".
Hit wave
Turbulence displacement at the cut. Wave distortion hides the swap. Heavier render — keep for moments that earn it.

Energetic

Whip pan
SVG directional motion blur smears horizontally at peak velocity. Seamless — no visible seam between panels.
Directional slide
Push transition, B shoves A off-screen.
Zoom punch
Scale into A, scale out on B. Beat-synced.
Warp-speed zoom
Radial star-streaks stretch outward + scale ramp + impact flash. Hyperspace punch-through.
Shake cut
3-frame handheld jolt, cut lands inside the shake. Impact, not dissolve.
Flash frame
1–2 frame pure-white stab hides the cut. Punchy, no ease.
Cross-zoom
A zooms up and blurs, B zooms down from big. Double-engine pop.

Title window transitions

Title rectangle window
Bold orange title sits over a bordered rectangle; the photo lives inside the rectangle and swaps A → B as the wipe. Type holds throughout. Inspired by Motion Array Minimal Title Transitions (594722).

Title blocks on colour
Two stacked accent-coloured blocks scaleX in from centre with a stagger, white type on top. Shot crossfades behind, blurred so the type stays readable. Editorial / corporate flavour.
Title stencil cut
Top half full-bleed footage; bottom half white card with the type stencilled out — the same footage shows through the letterforms. SVG mask drives the cut. A → B swaps in both halves simultaneously.

Title photo split
Top half video, bottom half white card with type. Top shot slides off left as shot B slides in from right; type breathes in / out via letter-spacing easing. Editorial magazine feel.

Title frame grow
Bordered rectangle scales 0 → 1 with overshoot, holds, then shrinks back. Type fades in inside the frame; sub-tagline types in via steps() — editorial nod to the typewriter pattern. Photo swap mid-cycle.

Reverse-action variant: type starts huge filling the frame, shrinks to its resting size while the photo behind expands from 70% to full. Cinematic punctuation; not in the source pack — added for rhythm variety.

Cinematic

Dip to white
Time jump, memory, reveal. Hold the peak 1–2 frames.
Dip to black
End of chapter. Weight, gravity. Don't overuse.
Morph cut
Crossfade + micro scale + slight blur. Hides jump cuts.
Light leak
Real Kling light-leak footage, screen blend. Analogue, nostalgic, film-burn warmth.
Liquid wipe
SVG turbulence displacement — organic, gooey reveal.
Shatter
Crack lines flash, white impact, then shards radiate from the break point and fly off-frame. Mirror-break reveal.
Ink bleed
Turbulence-distorted circle mask. Watercolour seeps across paper.
Smoke dissolve
Turbulence cloud rolls across, cut hides under the peak. Drifts off to reveal B.

Smooth dissolves (P2.13b additions)

Bokeh defocus dissolve
Both shots blur heavily through the cut + brightness lift, dissolve happens in the soft state, then sharpen back. Different from morph-cut (which holds focus). Cinematic memory / dream beat.
Pixel mosaic dissolve
12×7 grid of black tiles flicker in randomised order to occlude the cut, then back out to reveal B. Reads as low-res dissolve / digital fade. AE: Mosaic effect with random opacity offsets per tile.
Particle scatter dissolve
~50 luminous particles bloom out from the frame at the cut point, both shots blur and crossfade behind them. Different from smoke-dissolve (which is turbulence cloud). AE: Trapcode Particular emitter on the cut frame.
Lens-flare wipe
Hot anamorphic flare passes left-to-right across the frame; horizontal streak peaks at centre, hides the cut. Different from light-leak (ambient overlay). Premium / product-launch / sport.
Stock transitions from plugin packs are the fastest way to look like a template. If you use one, retime it, recolour it, trim the default stingers. Better still — build from primitives: a mask sweep, a clip-path, a translate with blur. Every transition should do a job. If it doesn't, it's a cut.
