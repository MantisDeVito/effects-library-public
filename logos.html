Logos

The professional logo reveal is always three beats: anticipation → reveal → settle. Beat 1 is what makes it feel designed — everyone skips it. Five variants below, all driving the same vectorised ACME placeholder wordmark so you can compare the feel of each reveal without the type changing.

Three-beat logo reveal

Anticipation, reveal, settle. The shared wordmark lets you pick the personality — particles feel tech/editorial, stamp feels sport/impact, iris feels cinematic, chroma feels modern/music, pour feels crafted/luxury.

Particle gather

Beat 1: particles gather (0–32%). Beat 2: letters snap in w/ 108% overshoot (32–48%). Beat 3: sheen sweeps, TM pops in at the top-right (50–100%).

Stamp impact

Beat 1: shockwave ring charges (0–32%). Beat 2: letters slam from above at 190% scale, drop to 100% with bounce (32–50%). Beat 3: ring dissipates, TM stamps in at the top-right (50–100%).

Iris reveal

Beat 1: center pulse + ring expand (0–32%). Beat 2: circular clip opens over the letters (32–48%). Beat 3: ring fades past the frame, TM pops in at the top-right (50–100%).

Chromatic lock

Beat 1: red/cyan channels drift apart, blurred (0–32%). Beat 2: channels slam back to center, the white letters snap sharp (42–52%). Beat 3: drop-shadow bloom fades, TM pops in at the top-right (50–100%).

Liquid pour

Beat 1: a drop falls from above and lands on the surface line (0–34%). Beat 2: letters fill top-to-bottom via a clip-path wipe (34–54%). Beat 3: surface line fades, TM pops in at the top-right as the final flourish (60–100%).

More reveals

Seven additional reveal flavours added in P2.13. Same three-beat shape, same shared ACME wordmark — different signature motion. Use the brush / streak / glitch ones for editorial and music; perimeter / mask / geometric for corporate; kinetic for high-impact opens.

Brush stroke

Accent streak passes left-to-right; letters wipe in behind the leading edge with a slight skew. Editorial / kinetic flavour. AE: clip-path on a precomp via Set Matte synced to a brushed shape layer.

Geometric build

Three accent stripes scaleX in cascading 200ms apart, dim down as the wordmark fades in over them. Confident corporate. AE: shape-layer scale-X keyframes with sequential offset; opacity ramp on the wordmark.

Light-streak pass

A wide light bar passes across the frame; the wordmark blooms into focus as the streak crosses centre. Premium / product-launch flavour. AE: gradient layer with screen blend, motion-blur the streak.

Glitch reveal

RGB channels (red + green) jump-cut between offset positions and clip slices for 1 second, then snap-resolve to the white wordmark with a brief glow. Music / editorial / hacker. Different pattern from chromatic-lock — slice-based, stepped.

Line draw perimeter

Single continuous stroke traces the rectangular frame around the wordmark in one motion, then the wordmark fades in inside. Frame fades out after. AE: trim-paths on a rectangle, cubic ease.

Vertical mask wipe

A glowing vertical bar sweeps across the frame; the wordmark wipes in from the leading edge. Sharper, cleaner cousin of the brush stroke. AE: linear wipe with thin highlight bar precomped on top.

ACME GROUP

Kinetic type into mark

Phrase scales in big and blurry, sharpens at peak, then collapses inwards into the small final wordmark. High-impact opener for product or campaign launches. AE: scale + tracking + blur keyframes on a text layer, swap to mark on the collapse.

This is a reference, not a template. Take the values, tighten them by eye in AE, and remember the skill brief: one signature effect executed precisely beats three effects fighting each other.
