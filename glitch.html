Glitch

Analogue failure modes turned into design. Use sparingly — once per video max for datamosh. CRT scanlines and chromatic aberration are the signatures to reach for when you need Y2K, retro, or "this signal is not okay". Every glitch should be motivated by a cut, a beat, or a revelation.

Analogue tube

CRT
Four-layer tube look — soft-rounded barrel body, phosphor bloom on bright pixels, drifting scanlines, and interlace shimmer. Stack-and-tune; each layer adds to the conviction.
Rolling scan
Brightness band sweeping top to bottom. Loose sync glitch.
VHS jitter
Sub-pixel tracking error. Step function, never linear.

Corruption

Static noise
Real per-frame grain. Screen blend lifts the highlights.
Dead pixels
Single-px flickers. Subtle — the eye catches them anyway.
Datamosh-look
Horizontal slab displacement. Fake codec smear.
Pixel sort-look
Neon streaks stretch downward from bright triggers. Lengths pulse, streaks blink in and out.
Pixel sort (classic)
Horizontal-row pixel sort by luminance — bright spans become smooth gradient streaks. The Kim Asendorf 2010 glitch-art look. AE: not native — best done in Photoshop's Pixel Sort filter or directly in code. Distinct from the neon-streaks card above.
ASCII
Per-frame ASCII rendering — denser glyphs for darker cells, sparse for lighter. The terminal / hacker / Matrix aesthetic. Cell size tunes resolution; charset tunes density.
Cable unplug
Flicker → squash to a 1px line → black → line returns → expands back.
Time-freeze ripple
Shockwave of a hue-shifted, zoomed copy of the frame travels outward — no drawn rings.
Once per video max for datamosh. Twice for chromatic aberration on a hero moment. CRT scanlines can live over an entire sequence if the brief earns it. Every glitch is a decision, not a default filter. Glitch where the signal is supposed to fail — a reveal, a cut to the villain, a lyric punch.
