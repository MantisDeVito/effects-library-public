Colours

Named colour grades, lighting moods, duotones, and animated colour moves. Every card is a before/after — hover any tile to see the grade fade in over the raw footage. Click to open fullscreen. Cinematic looks with iconic references so you know what you're stealing.

Cinematic Looks

Iconic named grades from film history. Hover to see the grade apply. These aren't filters — they're decisions a colourist made to serve a story.

Teal & Orange

The Hollywood blockbuster grade. Shadows pushed to teal, highlights to warm orange — skin tones pop against cool backgrounds. AE: Lumetri → Colour Wheels, or Curves → R/G/B. Ref: Mad Max: Fury Road.

Bleach Bypass

Desat -70%, contrast +75%. Crushed blacks, blown highlights, metallic feel — like the silver was left in the emulsion. AE: Lumetri → Saturation low, Contrast high. Ref: Saving Private Ryan, Seven.

Fincher Fade

Psychological desat -65% with a green-teal push into mids and highs. Cold, clinical, slightly unwell. AE: Lumetri Curves + Selective Colour → Greens. Ref: Zodiac, The Social Network, Mindhunter.

Sepia Vintage

Proper sepia matrix (0.393/0.349/0.272 — the Adobe standard) — not the flat CSS sepia(). Aged photo warmth for flashbacks and memory beats. AE: Lumetri → Creative → Camel, or Tint with #704214.

Lighting Moods

Grades that recreate the feeling of specific lighting conditions. Not film stocks — the colour of the light itself. Useful when the footage you shot doesn't match the scene you're trying to build.

Golden Hour

Warm romantic sunset, 2500–3500K. Reds and greens boosted, blues dropped -12%, saturation +18%, warm highlight glow on a soft-light blend. AE: Lumetri Temperature +25, Tint +5, radial warm wash. Ref: The Royal Tenenbaums.

Blue Hour

Post-sunset cool cast, 9000–12000K. Warms suppressed, blues boosted, light gradient overlay pulling the top into dusk. AE: Lumetri Temperature -40, Tint -10. Ref: twilight establishing shots, Malick dawns.

Neon Midnight

Saturation +75%, contrast +40%, brightness -15%, plus cyan + magenta radial bleed on a screen blend. Cyberpunk city at night. AE: Lumetri HSL → Neons + bloom pass. Ref: Blade Runner 2049, Drive.

Candlelight

Extreme warm shift (1500–1900K — one of the warmest natural sources). Reds boosted, greens neutralised, blues crushed, warm vignette pulling the edges into darkness. AE: Lumetri Temperature -100 + dark warm vignette. Ref: Barry Lyndon.

Gradient Maps

True luminance-to-colour remapping — every pixel's brightness picks a colour off a two-stop gradient. Editorial and poster design, not photography. Each card uses real feColorMatrix + feComponentTransfer, the same stack Spotify uses for its duotone covers.

Indigo → Amber

Two-stop duotone. Shadows become indigo #1a1a2e, highlights become amber #f5d27a. Elegant, editorial, timeless. AE: Tint with two colours, or Gradient Map. Pairs with serif type.

Risograph Plum

Duotone remap (plum #3d1c45 → cream #ffe8a8) plus a printed halftone dot pattern on a multiply blend. Zine and riso-print aesthetic. AE: Tint + Omino Halftone, or CC Ball Action at tiny size.

Animated Colour

Dynamic colour moves — the grade itself animates. No hover needed, these auto-loop. Use sparingly: animated colour is hypnotic but easy to overdo.

Hue Drift

Slow 360° hue rotation over 12 seconds. Trippy, meditative, dream-sequence territory. AE: Hue/Saturation → Master Hue with a linear expression, or Colorama. Works on any colourful footage.

Saturation Punch

Rhythmic saturation pulse at ~100bpm — normal → 2.0× → normal every 600ms. Beat-sync colour energy. AE: Lumetri Saturation keyframed to audio peaks, or Amplitude to Slider expression.

LUT recipes

Named grading recipes you can pull off the shelf for a job. Each card is a CSS-filter approximation of a real LUT — close enough for in-browser preview and direction discussions; for production, take the parameter set as a starting point in Lumetri / Resolve. Hover to apply the grade.

Nordic Cool

Desaturated low-temp grade with a cool blue cast and lifted shadows. Reads as Nordic / Scandi / restrained. AE: Lumetri Saturation -38, Temperature -25, Lift +10. Filter: saturate(0.62) hue-rotate(-12deg).

Faded Film

Lifted blacks, bleached saturation, slight paper warmth. The "shot on expired Kodak" look without leaning fully sepia. AE: Lumetri Lift +12, Saturation -22, Contrast -18. Filter: saturate(0.78) contrast(0.82) sepia(0.18) brightness(1.06).

Warm Document

Neutral skin tones with warm shadows — the "interview the founder" corporate documentary grade. Subtle, professional, never showy. AE: Lumetri Temperature +8, Saturation -5, Curves slight S. Filter: saturate(0.95) sepia(0.10) contrast(1.06).

Vintage Film Stock

Kodak Vision-style chromatic curves — slightly elevated saturation, sepia-bias mids, vignetted edges. Memory / nostalgia / heritage brand. AE: Lumetri Creative → Camel + warm vignette. Filter: saturate(1.18) sepia(0.22) hue-rotate(-8deg) contrast(1.12).

Moody Cinematic

Low-key, crushed blacks, selective saturation. Big-budget brooding — drama, tension, prestige. AE: Lumetri Saturation -45, Brightness -18, Contrast +38. Filter: saturate(0.55) brightness(0.82) contrast(1.38).

Urban High-Contrast

Punchy mid-tones, slight cyan in shadows, full saturation. Streetwear, sport, music-video energy. AE: Lumetri Saturation +30, Contrast +42, Curves R/G boosted in shadows. Filter: saturate(1.30) contrast(1.42) brightness(1.04).

Sun-Kissed

Warm highlights + lifted shadows + radial sun glow. Travel, summer, wellness, lifestyle. AE: Lumetri Highlights warm +20, Whites +12, radial warm wash. Filter: saturate(1.22) brightness(1.10) sepia(0.10).

High-Contrast Drama

Heavy S-curve, deep saturation, slight green-shadow shift. Closer to a music video or fashion editorial than a doco. AE: Lumetri Curves heavy S, Selective Colour shadows green +8. Filter: saturate(0.85) contrast(1.65) brightness(0.92) hue-rotate(3deg).

Photo treatments

Texture and finish effects that sit in colour territory but aren't grades. Halftone turns any image into a printed dot-grid reproduction. Orton glow is the wedding-film dreamy bloom — blurred bright copy layered with screen blend.

Halftone

Real halftone reproduction — dot diameter modulated by local luminance, paper background. Live-driven from the video so the dots redistribute every frame. The newspaper / zine / Saul Bass look. AE: Omino Halftone or CC Ball Action at small block size. Cell size tunes coarseness.

Edge detection (Sobel)

3×3 Sobel kernel on the luminance channel, applied per video frame. Every gradient becomes a line, every flat patch goes black. Editorial / TIWH stylisation pass; great as a transition state on the way to a graded shot. AE: Find Edges or CC Glass with low displacement; canvas here is the same maths.

Bayer dither

Ordered 8×8 Bayer matrix quantises each pixel to a 1-bit ink/paper palette. The classic newspaper-print and zine reproduction look. Visible matrix grain is the giveaway. Pairs with Risograph Plum.

Floyd-Steinberg dither

Error-diffusion dither — quantisation error gets distributed to neighbouring pixels in a 4-tap kernel. More organic than Bayer; reads photographic at small palette sizes. Same 1-bit ink/paper palette here.

Orton glow

Blurred bright copy layered over the footage with screen blend. Dreamy, romantic — the wedding-film bloom. AE: duplicate, blur heavily, brighten, screen blend at ~75%.

Colour is the first thing the audience feels and the last thing they can name. Pick one look per sequence and commit — a scene that can't decide between Teal & Orange and Fincher Fade reads as neither. Every grade here has an iconic film behind it; if you can't name the reference, you're not stealing hard enough.
